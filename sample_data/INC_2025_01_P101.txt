INCIDENT & ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS REPORT: INC-2025-014
Date of Incident: 2025-06-02
Time of Outage: 03:15 AM
Equipment ID: P-101 (Crude Transfer Pump)
Unit: Unit 12 - Distillation Yard
Incident Lead: R. Vance (Operations Manager)

INCIDENT DESCRIPTION:
At 03:15 AM, high hydrocarbon leak detector alarm triggered near P-101. Local operator observed liquid crude leaking from mechanical seal housing at a rate of ~15 liters/min. Emergency shutdown (ESD) activated remotely by Console Officer. Total unplanned downtime: 14.5 hours.

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS (RCA):
1. Immediate Cause: Primary mechanical seal face fractured due to thermal shock and dry running.
2. Root Cause: Seal flush plan API Plan 11 line was choked with particulate debris from un-cleaned suction strainer S-101. Blockage starved the seal cavity of cooling lubrication.
3. Contributing Factor: Strainer differential pressure gauge DP-101 was out of calibration and failed to alert operators of high differential pressure across S-101 prior to failure.

CORRECTIVE & PREVENTIVE ACTIONS (CAPA):
- Replaced damaged mechanical seal assembly on P-101.
- Flushed seal piping Plan 11 and cleaned strainer S-101.
- Updated SOP-LOTO-204 to mandate seal flush line flow verification prior to pump start.
- Calibrated differential pressure transmitter DP-101.
