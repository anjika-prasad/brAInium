INCIDENT & ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS REPORT: INC-2025-032
Date of Incident: 2025-07-11
Time of Outage: 14:40 PM
Equipment ID: P-101 (Crude Transfer Pump)
Unit: Unit 12 - Distillation Yard
Incident Lead: S. Kumar (Senior Reliability Engineer)

INCIDENT DESCRIPTION:
Crude Transfer Pump P-101 tripped on High Bearing Temperature (DE Bearing > 95°C). Secondary trip activated high vibration alarm at 7.1 mm/s RMS. Pump shut down automatically. Total unplanned downtime: 8.0 hours.

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS (RCA):
1. Immediate Cause: Catastrophic spalling of inboard drive-end ball bearing rollers (SKF 6314-C3).
2. Root Cause: Accelerated bearing wear resulting from uncorrected shaft misalignment following emergency seal replacement in June (Incident INC-2025-014).
3. Contributing Factor: Recommendations from inspection report IR-2025-0589 (which flagged bearing vibration degradation at 4.2 g's) were deferred due to high production demand.

CORRECTIVE & PREVENTIVE ACTIONS (CAPA):
- Replaced drive-end bearing SKF 6314-C3 and non-drive end bearing SKF 6312.
- Completed precision laser alignment of shaft (P-101 motor M-101 coupling offset < 0.01mm).
- Mandated that no vibration warning from IR reports can be deferred beyond 30 days without Chief Engineer approval.
